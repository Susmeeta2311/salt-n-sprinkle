My Mom’s Coconut Cake Recipe

HOW TO MAKE MY MOM’S FAMOUS COCONUT CAKE

  1. Beat the cake mix, butter, water, and eggs for 4 minutes, until batter is smooth.
  2. Grease two 8” or 9” cake pans, and divide the batter between the pans.
  3. Bake at 350 F for 25 minutes.
  4. Cool cakes completely, then slice in half.
  5. In a large bowl, beat together the sugar, sour cream and shredded coconut.
  6. Reserve 1 cup of mixture for frosting.
  7. Stack cake layers, brushing each layer liberally with coconut milk and 1/3 of the filling mixture.
  8. Combine the reserved cup of filling mixture with the Cool Whip.
  9. Spread on top and sides of cake.
  10. Optional: Sprinkle with additional shredded coconut.
  11. Seal in an airtight container and refrigerate for 2-3 days before serving.

INGREDIENTS

  • Golden butter cake mix (like Duncan Hines)
  • Butter
  • Water
  • Eggs
  • Granulated sugar
  • Sour cream
  • Shredded coconut, unsweetened
  • Coconut milk
  • Cool Whip

DO I REALLY HAVE TO REFRIGERATE THE CAKE FOR 2 DAYS?

  • I’ll be honest with you… YES. I mean, no, you don’t HAVE to refrigerate this cake for 2-3 days before you eat it. This cake will taste delicious the second you finish making it, but something magical does happen when you let it sit in the fridge for a bit. The flavors just come together in a crazy way that you just have to taste to believe.

  • So, I strongly urge you to have patience! Trust the process and refrigerate this coconut cake for a few days before diving in. Just trust me :)
