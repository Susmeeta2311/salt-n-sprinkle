Caramel Chai Upside Down Apple Cake

Tell Me More About This Cake!

  • I don’t drink coffee, so I love drinking tea. The inspiration for this caramel chai upside down cake comes from the warm, spiced flavors that come from a cup of fresh chai. The key ingredients for chai are cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, allspice, and cardamom, and we use those very same spices for this tasty apple cake.

  • If you’re like me and you worry that you’re gonna ruin your upside down cake when you turn it over only to reveal some soggy mess inside…you don’t have to worry about that with this easy recipe.

  • All you have to do is let the cake cool to room temp for about 15 minutes. When you flip it, you’ll get the most perfect apple upside down cake! And be sure to drizzle some extra caramel on top. Trust me!

INGREDIENTS

  1. Caramel
    • 4 tbsp butter (sub: vegan butter)
    • 1/4 c honey
  1. Cake
    • 2 1/2 c almond flour
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 2 tsp chai spice*
    • pinch of salt
    • 1/4 c coconut oil, melted
    • 2 eggs
    • 1/4 c honey
    • 1/4 c maple syrup
    • 2 tsp vanilla
  1. Apples
    • 2-3 large apples, cored and sliced about 1/4" thick**

How To Make This Upside Down Apple Cakes

  • Caramel
    1. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Whisk in the honey and the maple syrup. Allow it to come to a simmer and continue mixing as it bubbles, about 3-4 minutes. This will thicken the caramel.
    3. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.
  • Cake
    1. Preheat oven to 350 F. Line the bottom of an 8" or 9" round baking pan with parchment paper and set aside.
    2. In a large bowl, whisk together the almond flour, baking soda, chai spice, and salt.
    3. In a separate medium bowl, mix together the coconut oil, eggs, honey, maple syrup, and vanilla.
    4. Add the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring it together until it forms a batter. The batter will be thick. Set aside.
    5. Arrange the sliced apples along the bottom of the baking pan, overlapping them slightly. You may not need all of the apples.
    6. Pour the caramel over the apples. You may not need all of the caramel for this step. You only want to cover the bottom of the pan.
    7. Spread the cake batter on top of the apples, covering them completely.
    8. Bake for about 30-35 minutes, until the cake is lightly browned and the center is firm to the touch.
    9. Remove the cake from the oven and cool on a rack for about 15 minutes.
    10. Invert the cake on the cooling rack, and carefully remove the baking pan. Drizzle with the remaining caramel and serve.
