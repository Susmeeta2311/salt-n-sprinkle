Vegan Coconut Curry Soup

What can I serve with this coconut curry soup?

The best thing about this coconut curry soup recipe is that it’s so versatile! You can customize this soup base with whatever toppings you have on hand, and it’s hard to pick the wrong ones. Go with whatever veggies or proteins that you’ve been wanting to use up. I promise you can’t go wrong, but here are a few ideas:

  • Veggies: kale, cabbage, zucchini, carrots, mushrooms, squash, arugula, tomato
  • Protein: tofu, chickpeas, butter beans
  • Herbs: cilantro, parsley, basil, mint

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 onion, rough chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1" ginger, chopped
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp coconut sugar
  • 1 large sweet potato, cubed
  • 4 carrots, cubed
  • 2 cans coconut milk
  • 2 c vegetable broth
  • optional toppings: tofu, cabbage, mushroom, lime, cilantro, mint, lime

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger to the pot. Cook until soft, fragrant, and the onion just starts to brown, about 4 minutes.
  3. Add in the red curry paste and seasonings. Cook for 2 more minutes.
  4. Now add the sweet potatoes, carrots, vegetable broth, and coconut milk. Stir and bring to a simmer. Cook the sweet potatoes and carrots until soft, about 15 minutes. Be sure to stir and scrape the bottom of the pan.
  5. Allow soup to cool slightly before transferring to a blender (or, if you have an immersion blender, even better)! Blend the soup until it is smooth and creamy.
  6. Top with tofu or extra veggies!
